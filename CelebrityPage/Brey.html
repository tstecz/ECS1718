Mike Brey

Early Life

Michael Paul Brey was born on March 22nd, 1959. He was born in Bethesda, Maryland. His mother, Betty Brey, was an olympic freestyle swimmer. Brey attended DeMatha Catholic High School in Hyatsville, Maryland, and then attended Northwestern State University for three years of College, where he played basketball. Then, he attended and graduated from George Washington, where he played basketball and won MVP in the 80-81 season.

Career

After graduating College, Mike Brey returns to his Former High school, and becomes the assistant coach for the basketball team. Then, in 1987, Brey assistant coached Duke under Mike Krzyzewski. He did that for eight years, and then head coached the University of Delaware, where his record was 99-51. In 2000, Mike Brey became the head coach of The University of Notre Dame, where he has a record of 383-190.
